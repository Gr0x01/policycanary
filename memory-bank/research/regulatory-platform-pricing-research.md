# Regulatory/Compliance Research Platform Pricing Research

**Date**: 2026-03-03
**Purpose**: Benchmark pricing for regulatory intelligence, enforcement databases, and compliance research platforms to inform Policy Canary's tier pricing strategy.

---

## 1. FDA/Regulatory Enforcement Databases

### Redica Systems (formerly FDAzilla)
- **A la carte documents**:
  - FDA 483 report: **$289 per document** (instant download)
  - FDA 483 Response (483R): **$289 per document**
  - Establishment Inspection Report (EIR): **$1,190 per document**
- **Full platform (Redica Intelligence Cloud)**: Enterprise pricing, not published
  - Estimated **~$98,000/year average** based on $19.6M revenue / 200+ customers
  - Serves 19 of top 20 pharma companies, 9 of 10 top med device companies
  - Target: Large pharma, biotech, medical device companies
  - Raised $30M Series B -- this is a high-end enterprise play

### FDA Tracker AI (by Leucine)
- **Free tier**: Appears to be free access to 483 database and investigator insights
- **Premium**: Pricing not published; freemium model with email capture
- **Business model**: Lead generation funnel for Leucine's broader pharma AI platform
- **Note**: This is the closest competitor to what we're building in terms of approach (free/low-cost FDA data access) but focused on pharma, not food/supplements

### Registrar Corp
- **Facility 360 monitoring**: **$390/year** ($32.50/mo) -- registration monitoring + compliance tracking + shipment monitoring
- **Registration monitoring only**: **$195/year** ($16.25/mo) -- alerts for registration status changes
- **ComplyHub (FSVP/supplier compliance)**: Enterprise pricing, not published
  - One case study claims customer saved $16,000/year in manual compliance costs
  - Another saved $50,000+ annually in penalties avoided
- **Target**: Food importers, manufacturers needing FDA registration/FSVP compliance

### Complizen AI (FDA medical device focused)
- **Free plan**: Basic AI-powered FDA compliance exploration
- **Plus plan**: Per-seat pricing, monthly or annual billing
  - Range cited: **Free to $2,000/month** depending on features and team size
- **Focus**: Medical device 510(k) submissions, not food/supplements

## 5. Pricing Gap Analysis: Monitoring vs. Research Platform

### Observable Patterns

| Platform | Monitoring/Alerts Tier | Full Research/Database Tier | Multiplier |
|----------|----------------------|---------------------------|------------|
| Registrar Corp | $195/yr (alerts only) | ComplyHub (enterprise, est. $5K-15K/yr) | ~25-75x |
| FoodDocs | EUR 84/mo (basic) | EUR 250/mo (professional) | 3x |
| Nimonik | $300/mo (audit module) | $800+/mo (full registers) | 2.5-3x |
| Westlaw | $96/mo (essentials) | $566/mo (Edge Premium) | 5.9x |
| LexisNexis | $114/mo (essential) | $324/mo (professional) | 2.8x |
| Regology | Free (AI assistant) | $1,700/user/mo (professional) | Infinite (freemium) |
| Gartner | $20K/yr (read-only) | $80-100K/yr (senior access) | 4-5x |
| PitchBook | $12K/yr (solo basic) | $70K/yr (enterprise) | 5.8x |

### Key Findings on Tier Multipliers

1. **Basic monitoring to mid-tier research**: Typically **2.5-3x** price increase
2. **Basic monitoring to full platform**: Typically **4-6x** price increase
3. **The jump from "alerts" to "searchable database + analysis"**: Usually **3-4x**
4. **The pattern**: Entry-level = alerts/monitoring; Mid = searchable database; Top = database + analysis + analyst access/AI insights

## 6. Pricing Recommendation Analysis

### Our Positioning Context
- **What we offer at $49/mo**: Monitoring, alerts, basic enforcement tracking
- **What the research tier would add**: Full searchable database, enforcement history, trend analysis, semantic search, AI-enhanced insights

### Comparable Products at ~$199/mo ($150 premium over monitoring)

| Product | Price | What You Get |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| FoodDocs Standard | ~$181/mo | Food safety monitoring system, smart app, alerts, audit templates |
| LexisNexis Enhanced | $257/mo | Multi-state legal research, enhanced content |
| Westlaw Edge (single state) | $194/mo | AI-enhanced legal research, single jurisdiction |
| YouCompli | $300+/mo | Healthcare regulatory compliance |
| Nimonik (Newsletter + Library) | ~$1,000/mo | Regulatory library + newsletter alerts |

### What Competitors Charge for FDA-Specific Database Access

The direct FDA enforcement database market has a notable gap:

1. **Free/freemium**: FDA Tracker AI (limited, pharma-focused)
2. **Per-document**: Redica/FDAzilla ($289-$1,190 per document)
3. **Enterprise only**: Redica full platform (~$98K/year), AgencyIQ ($25-75K/year)
4. **Nothing in the $100-$500/month range for FDA food/supplement-specific enforcement intelligence**

This is the gap Policy Canary can fill.

### Price Point Assessment: $199/month

**Arguments that $199/month is appropriate:**
- 3-4x multiplier over $49 monitoring tier = within industry norms (4x)
- Sits in the "dead zone" between free FDA tools and $25K+ enterprise platforms
- Comparable to LexisNexis Essential ($114/mo) to Enhanced ($257/mo) jump
- More affordable than buying even ONE Redica 483 document ($289)
- Offers significantly more value than Registrar Corp's $390/year monitoring
- Priced for SMB compliance managers, QA directors, consultants -- not enterprise

**Arguments that $199/month might be TOO LOW:**
- AgencyIQ charges $25-75K/year for FDA intelligence (but targets different buyer)
- Westlaw Edge Premium is $566/month for legal research
- If the database genuinely replaces manual FOIA requests and per-document purchases, the value could support $299-$399/month
- Consultants who charge $200-400/hr would happily pay $299-499/month for a tool that saves them hours of research

**Arguments that $199/month might be TOO HIGH:**
- Registrar Corp's monitoring is only $390/YEAR
- Many SMB food companies are price-sensitive
- FoodDocs Professional is EUR 250/month and includes operational tools, not just data
- The buyer at a small supplement company may not have budget authority above $200/month

### Recommended Tier Structure

Based on this research, a three-tier approach:

| Tier | Price | What It Includes | Comparable To |
|------|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Monitor** | $49/month | Alerts, basic tracking, watchlists | Registrar Corp Facility 360 ($33/mo) but much better |
| **Research** | $149-$199/month | Full database, search, enforcement history, basic trends | Westlaw Essentials ($96-194/mo), LexisNexis Essential ($114/mo) |
| **Intelligence** | $349-$499/month | Everything + AI analysis, trend reports, semantic search, competitor monitoring, consultant-grade tools | Westlaw Edge ($194-566/mo), Nimonik ($500+/mo) |

**My recommendation: $199/month is defensible and well-positioned, but consider testing $249/month.**

Rationale:
- $199 feels like "monitoring + some extras" to sophisticated buyers
- $249 signals "this is a real research platform" without hitting enterprise pricing anxiety
- The $49 to $249 jump is a 5x multiplier -- within the 4-6x range seen at Westlaw, PitchBook, and Gartner
- Still far below the $25K+ that AgencyIQ and Redica charge enterprise buyers
- A single Redica 483 document costs $289 -- your annual plan at $249/mo ($2,988/yr) gives unlimited access to tagged, searchable enforcement data
- Consider an annual discount: $199/mo billed annually ($2,388/yr) vs $249/mo monthly
