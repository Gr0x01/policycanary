## 1. MoCRA (Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act)

### What MoCRA Requires

MoCRA is the most significant expansion of FDA authority over cosmetics since the FD&C Act was passed in 1938. Signed into law December 29, 2022, it introduces:

- **Facility registration** for all domestic and foreign facilities that manufacture or process cosmetics distributed in the U.S.
- **Product listing** with FDA, including ingredient lists, product categories, and responsible person information
- **Adverse event reporting** (mandatory serious adverse event reporting within 15 business days)
- **Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)** compliance (rulemaking pending)
- **Fragrance allergen labeling** (rulemaking pending)
- **Safety substantiation** requirements
- **Mandatory recall authority** for FDA (new power)
- **PFAS assessment** reporting

### Small Business Exemptions

Companies with average gross annual sales of cosmetic products under $1 million over the previous 3 years are exempt from some requirements. However, exemptions do NOT apply to products that:
- Contact the mucous membrane of the eye
- Are injected
- Are intended for internal use
- Alter appearance for more than 24 hours

### Timeline of Deadlines (2023-2027+)

#### COMPLETED Deadlines

| Deadline | Requirement | Status |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Dec 29, 2023 | Product listing for existing cosmetics marketed before MoCRA | PASSED |
| June 29, 2024 | Fragrance allergen labeling proposed rule (original target) | MISSED by FDA |
| July 1, 2024 | Facility registration enforcement begins | PASSED - ENFORCED |
| July 1, 2024 | Product listing submissions required | PASSED - ENFORCED |
| Dec 29, 2024 | Responsible Person name/address on packaging | PASSED |
| Dec 29, 2024 | GMP proposed rule (original target) | MISSED by FDA |

#### UPCOMING Deadlines

| Deadline | Requirement | Status |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Dec 29, 2025 | FDA must publish PFAS assessment report | DUE (may be delayed) |
| May 2026 | Fragrance allergen labeling proposed rule (revised target) | EXPECTED |
| July 1, 2026 | First biennial facility registration renewal | UPCOMING - KEY DATE |
| Late 2026+ | GMP final rule | DELAYED - classified as "long-term action" |
| 2027+ | Fragrance allergen labeling final rule (estimated) | DELAYED |

**Critical insight:** FDA has significantly delayed MoCRA rulemaking. GMP regulations are now classified as a "long-term action" with no proposed rule expected within 12 months. Contributing factors include FDA reorganization, staffing reductions under the Trump administration, and the executive order requiring agencies to remove 10 regulations for every new one.

### Scale of Impact

- **9,528 unique active facility registrations** as of January 1, 2025
- **589,762 unique active product listings** as of January 1, 2025
- **5,085 cosmetic & beauty products manufacturing businesses** in the U.S. (IBISWorld, 2025)
- Estimated **$39.3 billion** market size for cosmetic & beauty products manufacturing in the U.S. (2025)

### Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Mandatory product recalls (new FDA authority under MoCRA)
- Facility registration suspension
- Product listing termination
- Products classified as misbranded/adulterated
- Border holds on imports
- Seizure of products
- Injunctions to halt operations
- Significant fines
- Legal action by FDA

### Recent FDA Enforcement Under MoCRA

Enforcement has been relatively limited so far (early implementation phase):

- **December 18, 2025:** Warning letter to Private Label Skin Care, Inc. in Canoga Park, CA for CGMP violations found during June 2025 inspection, including distribution of adulterated OTC drug products with elevated impurities.
- **December 18, 2025:** FDA released draft guidance on "Questions and Answers Regarding Mandatory Cosmetics Recalls," clarifying the process for mandatory recalls of adulterated or misbranded cosmetics.
- **First two months of Trump administration:** FDA issued warning letters to three entities for potential MoCRA violations.
- FDA has adopted a compliance policy approach, issuing guidance before aggressive enforcement.

**Key takeaway for Policy Canary:** MoCRA enforcement is still ramping up. Companies are in a "compliance preparation" phase, which is the ideal time for a regulatory intelligence tool. The July 2026 facility registration renewal is the next major compliance event driving urgency.

## 2. FDA Supplement Enforcement Trends

### Enforcement Statistics (2024-2025)

- **73% increase in warning letters** (July 1 - Dec 3, 2025 vs. same period 2024): 327 warning letters issued
- **58 untitled letters in FY2025**, up dramatically from 5 in 2024 and 4 in 2023
- **1,540 more inspections** in food and cosmetics industries from 2024 to 2025
- **694 more inspections** total in FY2025 vs. FY2024
- **25% rise in warning letters** to non-compliant facilities over the last year (per FDA data)

### Supplement Inspection Failure Rates

**FY2023 data (most recent detailed breakdown):**

- **501 total inspections** (domestic + foreign)
- **48% of inspected firms received Form 483** (cited for violations)
- **52% had no violations** (261 inspections)
- **60% of foreign firms** cited (24 of 40 inspections across 17 countries)
- Average of ~5 cGMP observations per Form 483

**FY2024 data:**

- **46% increase in 21 CFR Part 111 observations** (1,578 in 2024 vs. 1,083 in 2023)
- Top violation remained the same: failure to establish product specifications for identity, purity, strength, and composition

### Top 5 CGMP Violations (Supplement Facilities)

| Rank | Violation | Regulation | % of 483s |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Product specifications not established | 21 CFR 111.70(e) | 33% |
| 2 | Component specifications not established | 21 CFR 170(b)(2) | 20% |
| 3 | Identity specifications not established | 21 CFR 170(b)(1) | 18% |
| 4 | QC written procedures missing | 21 CFR 111.103 | 17.5% |
| 5 | Holding/distribution procedures missing | 21 CFR 111.453 | 17.1% |

**Industry expert observation:** "The same cGMP violations cited by FDA a decade ago remain the top violations, suggesting minimal industry progress on compliance fundamentals."

### NAC Regulatory Status

- In 2022, after NPA filed a citizen petition and lawsuit, FDA issued a final guidance establishing enforcement discretion that allows NAC products back on the market as dietary supplements.
- NAC is currently sold as a dietary supplement under FDA's enforcement discretion policy.

### NMN Regulatory Status

- **September 29, 2025:** FDA reversed its position and confirmed NMN is lawful for use in dietary supplements, ending ~3 years of regulatory uncertainty.
- NMN remains classified as a **New Dietary Ingredient (NDI)** -- companies must submit NDI notifications before marketing.
- **December 2025:** FDA reinstated NDI status of NMN with new letters to ingredient suppliers.
- Each company marketing NMN must submit its own NDIN unless sourcing from a supplier with an existing filing.

### CBD/Hemp Regulatory Status

- **November 2025:** Congress enacted major changes through Continuing Resolution/Appropriations Package (Section 781).
- **Effective November 12, 2026:** New definition of hemp based on total THC content (not just delta-9 THC). Products capped at 0.4 mg total THC per container. Synthetic cannabinoids banned.
- Many hemp-derived CBD products currently on the market will become illegal under federal law after November 12, 2026.
- FDA maintains that THC and CBD products are excluded from the dietary supplement definition.
- Companies selling CBD products face a **compliance cliff** in November 2026.

### New Dietary Ingredient (NDI) Notification Trends

- **March 2024:** FDA released Final Guidance on NDI notification procedures and timeframes.
- **April 2024:** FDA issued draft guidance on NDI Notification Master Files.
- **2026 priority:** FDA stated intent to release final NDI guidance in 2026.
- Industry concerns about enforcement consistency and companies marketing products without proper NDINs.

## 4. Target Customer Validation

### What VP/Director of Regulatory Affairs Actually Does

**Day-to-day responsibilities:**
- Reviewing and approving product labels for FDA compliance (health claims, structure/function claims, nutrient content claims, allergen declarations, formatting)
- Managing GMP compliance per 21 CFR Part 111 (SOPs, production controls, records, complaint handling)
- Preparing and submitting NDI notifications to FDA
- Monitoring regulatory changes and interpreting impact on product portfolio
- Overseeing quality system development (specifications, stability programs, quality agreements)
- Serving as U.S. Agent for registered dietary supplement firms
- Managing FDA inspection readiness and responding to FDA 483s
- Reviewing marketing materials for claim compliance
- Tracking state-level regulations (e.g., Prop 65, NY age restrictions on supplements)

**Key gap observation:** Most existing tools are either (a) focused on one narrow function (labeling, quality management, or supply chain), (b) expensive enterprise platforms, or (c) designed for much larger companies. There is no affordable, AI-powered regulatory intelligence tool specifically designed for small-to-mid-size supplement, cosmetics, and food companies.

### Contract Manufacturer Compliance Burden

- Large contract manufacturers like Vitaquest produce **4,000+ custom formulas for 500+ brands**
- Compliance responsibility legally rests with the **brand owner**, not the manufacturer
- Brand owners often incorrectly assume their contract manufacturer handles all compliance
- Contract manufacturers face dual burden: their own facility compliance + supporting brand owner compliance
- FDA inspects the manufacturing facility regardless of who owns the brand

## 5. Industry Trends

### MAHA (Make America Healthy Again) and FDA Under Kennedy

The MAHA movement under HHS Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr. is the single biggest regulatory disruption to the food/supplement industry in a generation:

- **MAHA Commission** established by Executive Order 14212 (Feb 13, 2025)
- **GRAS pathway reform:** Kennedy directed FDA to consider eliminating the self-affirmation pathway for GRAS ingredients, which would require companies to publicly notify FDA of new ingredient use with safety data before market introduction
- **Red No. 3 ban:** FDA revoked authorization (Jan 15, 2025). Food manufacturers have until **January 15, 2027** to reformulate. Ingested drugs until January 18, 2028
- **All petroleum-based food dyes** targeted for elimination by end of 2027 (FD&C Green No. 3, Red No. 40, Yellow No. 5, Yellow No. 6, Blue No. 1, Blue No. 2)
- **Supplement industry deregulation signals:** Kennedy stated the administration would end FDA's "aggressive suppression" of vitamins and dietary supplements
- **Supplement industry expansion goals:** Industry aiming for bolder health claims, potential government/insurance coverage of supplements, and FSA/HSA eligibility
- **FDA Human Foods Program rebrand** and restructuring underway

**Net effect:** Massive regulatory uncertainty. Some areas are deregulating (supplements), while others face new requirements (food additives, GRAS). This is EXACTLY the environment where a regulatory intelligence tool provides the most value.

### Dietary Supplement Listing Act of 2026

- **Introduced January 15, 2026** by Senator Durbin (S.3677)
- Would require **mandatory product listing** with FDA for all dietary supplements
- Required information: product names, all ingredients, electronic label copy, allergen statements, health/structure-function claims
- Public electronic database of all dietary supplements
- Supported by CRN (Council for Responsible Nutrition), Consumer Federation of America, USP
- Opposed by NPA (Natural Products Association) as undermining DSHEA
- **Previous attempts failed** in 2022 and 2024
- If passed: would create massive compliance event for 100,000+ products

### FSMA Enforcement Updates

- **FSMA 204 (Food Traceability Rule):** Compliance date pushed from January 20, 2026 to **July 2028**
- **Reduced FDA inspection capacity:** Foreign food safety inspections fell to new lows in 2025 after budget cuts and mass layoffs. Monthly foreign inspections fell ~50% in March 2025, remaining ~30% lower through July
- **FY2026 appropriations:** $1.17 billion allocated to FDA Human Foods Program, with limits imposed on FSMA enforcement
- High-risk domestic facilities must be inspected every 3 years (statutory requirement)

### OTC Monograph Reform (CARES Act)

- OMUFA reauthorization for FY2026-2030 underway
- Proposed base revenue for FY2026: ~$36.5 million in user fees
- December 2025: FDA proposed first new sunscreen ingredient in decades
- FDA announced intent for 2026 public meeting on increasing OTC drug access
- **Relevance to Policy Canary:** Many cosmetic companies also sell OTC drugs (sunscreens, anti-acne, anti-dandruff). MoCRA + OTC reform creates dual compliance burden.

### Lab-Grown / Novel Food Ingredients

- Five cultivated meat companies now have FDA approval ("no questions" letters)
- State bans in Florida, Alabama, Mississippi, Montana, Indiana, Nebraska, Texas (effective Sept 2025)
- Classification challenges: cultivated meat doesn't fit existing FDA definitions
- USDA/FDA shared jurisdiction creates regulatory complexity
- Growing regulatory opportunity but niche market for now

### State-Level Supplement Regulation

- **New York (April 2024):** Age verification required for weight loss and muscle building supplements
- Similar measures advancing in Michigan, Illinois, Massachusetts, New Jersey
- Industry legal challenges failed; courts upheld state authority
- Creates patchwork compliance burden for national brands
