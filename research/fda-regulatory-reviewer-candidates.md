## Engagement Strategy

### Framing the Ask

Do not lead with "AI review." That framing invites skepticism or liability nervousness. Lead with:

> "We're building a regulatory intelligence product that synthesizes FDA regulatory changes into product-specific action items. Before expanding, we want an experienced FDA practitioner to audit our categorization logic, urgency assessments, and deadline accuracy against their own read of the regulations — essentially a quarterly sanity check from someone who lives in this space."

This frames the engagement as expert review of an intelligence product, not review of legal advice. It is lower-stakes for the reviewer and does not require them to endorse AI outputs as legal guidance.

### Suggested Outreach Order

1. **Marc Ullman** — Highest name recognition in supplement space. Of Counsel flexibility. Active speaker who may see this as an interesting engagement.
2. **Katherine Giannamore** — Most likely to say yes quickly. Small firm, right market segment, accessible.
3. **Kristen Klesh** — Best cross-sector coverage. May require more process to engage but worth pursuing for the MoCRA/cosmetics credibility.
4. **John Bailey** — Reach through EAS or LinkedIn directly for cosmetics-specific review. Complement Ullman's supplement depth.
5. **Benjamin England** — Strong candidate if import/customs compliance becomes a bigger data theme.

### Rate Expectations

| Profile | Estimated Hourly | Retainer Target |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Of Counsel / Boutique Partner | $350–$600/hr | $1,500–$3,500/mo |
| Small Firm Principal | $250–$450/hr | $1,000–$2,500/mo |
| Large Firm Partner | $600–$900/hr | Likely not feasible |
| Non-attorney Consultant (EAS advisors) | $200–$400/hr | $1,000–$2,000/mo |

A monthly retainer covering 3–5 hours of review work is a realistic ask. Frame it as: review a batch of AI-generated regulatory summaries once per month, flag categorization errors, note any urgency miscalibrations, provide a brief written assessment. Position the role as contributing to product accuracy and, implicitly, as a soft endorsement relationship once trust is established.
